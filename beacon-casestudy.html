AI Learning Agent

An AI-powered Slack agent that surfaces targeted learning resources in the flow of work, triggered by a completed feedback synthesis and anchored to the Success Model. Learning delivered where leaders already are.

01 · Problem

Learning that lives outside the workflow doesn't get used

The traditional model asks leaders to complete a feedback process, then separately navigate to an LMS, find relevant content, and apply it to a development conversation. Each step is a drop-off point. By the time a manager opens an LMS, the feedback moment has passed and the learning has lost its context.

The gap

Development resources exist. But they're decoupled from the moments when leaders are most motivated to use them: immediately after a meaningful feedback or calibration event.

The opportunity

Surface the right learning at the right moment, inside the tool the leader is already using, triggered by context, anchored to the specific competency gap identified in their feedback synthesis.

02 · Process

Designing Beacon

Beacon was designed around a single principle: the best learning moment is the one that happens without friction, at the point of need, in the tool the leader already trusts.

1
Trigger architecture
Beacon fires when a feedback synthesis is completed, not on a calendar cadence. Context-triggered delivery means the learning is immediately relevant rather than generically timed.
2
Privacy by design
The channel-level message references "Direct Report Convo 5.17.26," a timestamp, not a name. Identity is revealed only on deliberate click, creating a privacy boundary that mirrors how enterprise tools should handle sensitive talent data.
3
Three learning formats
Article (external research), Framework (structured model), Micro-lesson (Beacon-generated, competency-specific). Three formats serve different learning preferences and time availability. A leader with 3 minutes gets different value than one with 8.
4
Accountability loop
A progress tracker above the cards shows completion state at a glance. Each card cycles through Unread → In Progress → Complete → Reset — creating a lightweight accountability mechanism without requiring a separate tracking system.
5
Micro-lesson with reflection prompt
The Beacon-generated card includes an expandable micro-lesson and a manager reflection prompt, a question designed to be used in the next 1:1. Learning that ends with an action, not a checkbox.

Micro-lesson
At Stripe, Interpersonal Savvy isn't about being likeable — it's about being generative. The most influential senior ICs don't just do great work; they leave every interaction having made the people around them clearer, more capable, or more confident.

This is what Stripe calls exothermic: energy that releases outward, not inward. For a senior engineer, it looks like: naming what you've learned in a retro so others benefit, flagging your approach before you start rather than after you finish, or asking a PM what they need from you at the top of a planning session instead of waiting to be asked.

None of these require authority. All of them build it.

04 · Design decisions

Three choices that shaped Beacon

01
Context-triggered, not scheduled
Beacon fires when a feedback synthesis completes, not on a Monday morning cadence. The learning arrives when the leader's mind is already on the direct report, not when a calendar decides it should be.
02
Privacy boundary by design
Channel-level anonymization ("Direct Report Convo 5.17.26") protects identity at the team layer. Name revealed only on deliberate click, a deliberate UX choice that reflects how enterprise talent data should flow.
03
Accountability without overhead
The progress tracker and card status cycling create lightweight accountability — no new system, no separate dashboard. The conversation thread becomes the record.
05 · Reflection

What this work demonstrates

"Beacon isn't a learning tool that lives in Slack. It's a talent system that uses Slack as its delivery layer. The difference matters because it reduces the number of steps to find learning and development opportunities."

This artifact demonstrates the ability to think beyond content creation into ecosystem architecture: where learning surfaces, when it surfaces, and how it connects back to the frameworks and tools already in use. Beacon closes the loop between feedback synthesis and development action, turning a one-time manager task into a continuous, context-aware learning moment. That's the "in-platform enablement" the Stripe Learning Architect role is built around.
