The Engine

Two tools that operationalize the Success Model, turning a static framework into active talent infrastructure. The Feedback Synthesis Tool and the Succession Planning Tool share the same competency engine and tell a connected story.

Feedback Synthesis Tool
Case study 02 · Two versions: live API and simulated

Transforms raw, unstructured observations into competency-anchored strengths, opportunities, and recommended actions. The system prompt embeds the full Success Model so every output is genuinely anchored to the framework, not labeled after the fact. Available in two versions: a live API-powered tool and a pre-loaded simulation.

Learning Delivery · Case studies 04 + 05

The Ecosystem

Two artifacts. One ecosystem loop.

These aren't tools for their own sake. They're the infrastructure that turns a feedback moment into a structured, competency-anchored capability-building journey, delivered where leaders already work.

The most architecturally forward piece in the portfolio. Beacon closes the loop between feedback synthesis and development action, delivering learning in the flow of work, not redirected away from it.

Beacon: AI Learning Agent
Slack simulation · Context-triggered · In-platform enablement
Case study 04
Beacon is an AI agent created for this portfolio, as a way to connect feedback to action. Beacon fires when a feedback synthesis completes, surfacing three targeted learning resources directly into a Slack channel, in this case #people-insights. Privacy-by-design: the channel references "Direct Report Convo 5.17.26" while name and competency detail are revealed only on deliberate click. Three card formats (Article, Framework, Micro-lesson) serve different time availability and learning preferences. A progress tracker and card status cycling create lightweight accountability without a separate system.

A live application could also pull from integrated LMS, LXP, and knowledge base options.

Learning architect.
Systems thinker.
Builder.

I take a holistic approach to learning and enablement, considering systems design alongside perceived learning need. My focus is on how knowledge becomes usable in real workflows, how it shows up in decisions, behaviors, and the day-to-day work of managers and teams. I cringe over training for training's sake. I want to understand the root cause before any learning strategy is considered.

I draw on iterative design approaches from learning design, including agile cycles, to move quickly from ambiguity into structured direction without overbuilding upfront.

From there, I shift into rapid prototyping, developing lightweight, workflow-native experiences that can be tested early and refined through use. When possible, I rely on short feedback loops to understand what is actually landing in practice and adjust direction incrementally rather than through large, infrequent releases.

With AI as a companion and not a final sign-off, I embed it throughout my process as a practical tool for synthesis, structuring complex inputs, and accelerating how expert knowledge becomes something others can actually use.

I prioritize people and the user experience across every deliverable, and I do my best work in open dialogue, examining angles and pushing toward the strongest solution together.

A note for Stripe

Why this portfolio exists.

This portfolio is designed to demonstrate my belief that scaling human capability requires operating at the intersection of people strategy, learning architecture, and AI enablement. I view these elements not as adjacent disciplines, but as a single, interconnected system.

I used public-facing information on Stripe's mission and operating principles to design a fictional Stripe "Success Model" for this demonstration. I hope the liberties taken spark interest rather than ire. Mapping this model laid the structural foundation for the interpretive engine powering the tools and in-platform agents showcased here. As a system designer, looking at the total ecosystem means looking beyond isolated content builds. It means engineering a learning infrastructure embedded natively within daily user workflows, rather than detached training events.

The work here reflects a 10+ year track record of designing capability systems for high-growth global organizations, including enterprise leadership frameworks at Emerson, LMS/LXP ecosystem architecture at Pure Storage, and core talent infrastructure at Axon. Operating at the boundary of instructional design and learning "product management" cemented the question that drives my work: How do you take complex human knowledge and scale it without losing what makes it valuable?

The answer, increasingly, is AI-informed systems design. Not AI for its own sake, but AI as the mechanism that closes the gap between a framework and a behavior, between a feedback moment and a development action, between knowing what matters and making it stick.

This portfolio offers a high-fidelity glimpse of what that infrastructure looks like in practice. Let's build what comes next.
