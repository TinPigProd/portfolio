01 · Problem

Beacon surfaces the nudge. But what comes next?

Beacon delivers three learning cards in Slack at the moment of highest relevance. But cards are a starting point, not a system. For learning to change behavior at scale, the nudge needs to connect to a deeper infrastructure, a pathway that sequences content deliberately, tags it to the right skills, assigns it to the right audience, and tracks progress without requiring a manager to manually curate anything.

The gap

Point-in-time learning nudges don't build capability on their own. Without an underlying pathway architecture, there's no sequencing, no skill progression tracking, and no scalable way to ensure the right content reaches the right person at the right level.

The opportunity

Design the LXP pathway layer that Beacon draws from — showing how competency gaps identified in a feedback synthesis translate directly into a structured, skills-tagged, auto-assigned learning experience in the flow of work.

03 · Process

Designing the pathway architecture.

The pathway was designed around Jordan Mills' three flagged competencies: Collaborates, Communicates Effectively, and Interpersonal Savvy, using the same architectural decisions that govern real LXP implementations.

1
Competency-to-skill mapping
Each Success Model competency was mapped to a skill tag in the LXP taxonomy — creating the metadata layer that enables auto-assignment, discoverability, and progress tracking tied to the framework rather than arbitrary content categories.
2
Trigger rule architecture
The pathway fires automatically when Beacon's feedback synthesis identifies a gap in any of the three target competencies. The trigger connects the synthesis event to the LXP assignment without manual intervention from a manager or L&D team member.
3
Content sequencing decisions
Required content (3 items) establishes the minimum viable learning experience — article, framework, micro-lesson — each anchored to a specific competency and observable behavior from the Success Model. Optional enrichment (4 items) serves learners who want to go deeper without making the pathway feel mandatory and heavy.
4
Audience targeting rules
The pathway is scoped to Senior IC level in Engineering — not because all senior engineers need it, but because the trigger rule ensures it only reaches people whose synthesis identified these specific gaps. Precise targeting prevents noise.
5
Dual view design
Both the admin/architect view and the learner view were designed deliberately. The admin view shows the infrastructure logic — triggers, metadata, skill coverage, analytics. The learner view shows what Jordan actually sees: a clear why, a clean sequence, and skill progression made visible.

05 · Design decisions

Three choices that shaped the architecture.

01
Framework-native metadata
Every content item is tagged to a Success Model competency and tier, not a generic topic. This means the pathway is discoverable by skill gap, not by browsing — and analytics reflect framework progress, not just content consumption.
02
Required minimum, optional depth
Three required items establish the floor. Four optional items serve motivated learners without making the pathway feel heavy. Respects the leader's time while leaving room for genuine curiosity.
03
Both views, by design
Showing admin and learner views simultaneously demonstrates that good pathway design thinks from both directions — what the architect builds and what the learner experiences are equally important design surfaces.
06 · Reflection

What this work demonstrates.

"A learning nudge without infrastructure is a good intention. A pathway architecture is what makes the intention repeatable, measurable, and scalable."

This artifact closes the loop on the full talent development system demonstrated across this portfolio. The Success Model defines the language. The Feedback and Succession Tools identify the gaps. Beacon surfaces the moment. FictionalPig LXP provides the infrastructure that turns a single learning moment into a structured capability-building journey — anchored to the same competency framework throughout, auto-assigned without manual intervention, and designed to be experienced differently by the architect who builds it and the learner who lives it.
